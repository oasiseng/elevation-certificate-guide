# Elevation Certificate Resources and References

Comprehensive list of official FEMA resources, technical guidance, and support contacts for Elevation Certificates.

## FEMA Official Resources

### Elevation Certificate Forms and Tools

- **Elevation Certificate Form (FEMA 086-0-33)**
  Download the current fillable EC form and instructions from: agents.floodsmart.gov/fill-ec

- **Elevation Certificate Agent Portal**
  Insurance agents can access tools and resources for submitting ECs at: agents.floodsmart.gov

- **FEMA Flood Map Service Center**
  Interactive mapping tool for viewing current FIRMs, flood zones, and BFE data
  URL: msc.fema.gov/portal/home
  Phone: (877) 336-2627
  Hours: Monday–Friday, 7:30 a.m.–5:00 p.m. EST

- **FMIX Customer Care Center**
  Support for flood map inquiries and technical assistance
  Phone: (877) 336-2627
  Email: Ask-FEMA@fema.dhs.gov

### Insurance Rating and Discounts

- **FEMA Rate Explanation Guide**
  Detailed explanation of how Elevation Certificates and First Floor Height affect insurance rates under Risk Rating 2.0

- **FEMA Discount Explanation Guide**
  Information on mitigation discounts available when properties are elevated or floodproofed

- **Community Status Book**
  Community-by-community information on CRS ratings, FIRM adoption dates, and floodplain management standards
  URL: agents.floodsmart.gov/community-status-book

## FEMA Publications and Technical Guidance

### Primary Publications

- **FEMA MT-1 Technical Guidance (November 2024)**
  FEMA's current technical guidance for MT-1 requests, including LAG/grade interpretation, fill-based map change documentation, and application support requirements
  URL: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_guidance_mt-1_technical_guidance_nov_2024.pdf

- **FEMA Publication 467-1: Floodplain Management Bulletin: Elevation Certificate**
  Comprehensive technical guidance on EC completion, common errors, and use cases

- **FEMA Publication 348: Protecting Building Utilities from Flood Damage**
  Technical guidance on flood-resistant M&E placement and protection

### Technical Bulletins

- **Technical Bulletin 1-93: Openings in Foundation Walls and Elevated Buildings**
  Specifications for flood openings, sizing, and placement requirements

- **Technical Bulletin 2-93: Wet Floodproofing Requirements**
  Standards for wet floodproofing non-residential buildings and residential enclosures

- **Technical Bulletin 3-93: Wet Floodproofing Non-Residential Buildings**
  Detailed requirements for non-residential wet floodproofing design

- **Technical Bulletin 6-95: Six-Inch Elevation and Irregularly Sloped Lots**
  Guidance on minor elevations and sloped terrain situations

- **Technical Bulletin 11-01: Crawlspace Construction**
  Standards for properly constructed and vented crawlspaces, including measurement techniques

- **Technical Bulletin 12-01: Wet Floodproofing Residential Buildings**
  Standards for floodproofing residential basements under FEMA exception criteria

### Regulatory References

- **44 CFR Part 60: Floodplain Management Regulations**
  Federal regulations governing community floodplain management standards and NFIP participation

- **44 CFR Part 61: Insurance Coverage and Rates**
  Flood insurance rating rules and premium determination

## State NFIP Coordinators

Each state administers the NFIP in coordination with FEMA and maintains floodplain management programs. Contact your state's NFIP coordinator for:

- Surveyor and engineer licensing requirements for EC completion
- Community-specific floodplain management standards
- Elevation Certificate submission procedures
- Professional recommendations and referrals

**To find your state coordinator:** Contact FEMA Regional Office or visit fema.gov/state-coordinator

## How to Use These Resources

**For Homeowners:** Start with FEMA's consumer resources at FloodSmart.gov and your local floodplain manager's office.

**For Insurance Agents:** Use the Agent's Guide, Rate Explanation Guide, and agents.floodsmart.gov portal for EC submission and client support.

**For Floodplain Managers:** Reference 44 CFR Part 60, FEMA publications, and state coordinator guidance for community standards.

**For Engineers and Surveyors:** Consult FEMA technical bulletins, state licensing boards, and professional association standards for technical guidance.
