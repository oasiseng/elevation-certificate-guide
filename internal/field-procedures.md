# Field Procedures for Elevation Certificate Data Collection

**Template for engineering and surveying firms — customize for your operations**

---

## Overview

This document outlines the standard field procedures for collecting accurate elevation data for FEMA Elevation Certificates. These procedures are based on widespread industry practice and are designed to be customized by your firm to match your specific equipment, team structure, and regional conditions.

---

## Pre-Visit Preparation

### FIRM Data Review and Documentation

Before arriving at the site, pull all available Flood Insurance Rate Map (FIRM) information:

- **Community name and number** — Verify the NFIP Community ID
- **County and state** — Confirm jurisdiction
- **Map panel number and suffix** — Note the current effective FIRM panel
- **FIRM Index Date and Panel Effective Date** — Document the date your EC references
- **Flood zone designation** — Identify AE, A, AO, AR, X (shaded), X (unshaded), or other
- **Base Flood Elevation (BFE)** — Note BFE in feet and elevation datum (NGVD 29 or NAVD 88)
- **Source of BFE** — FIS, FIRM, Community Determined, or Other
- **LiMWA status** — Check if property lies in a Limited Detailed Study area
- **CBRS/OPA status** — Confirm if property is in Coastal Barrier Resources System or Otherwise Protected Area

*Tip: Many firms maintain a FIRM search tool (FEMA Map Service Center) bookmarked for quick lookups. Consider pre-loading community data into your project management system.*

### Property Verification

- **Legal owner name** — Cross-reference with property records
- **Current mailing address** — Verify the address you'll visit
- **Property description and tax parcel** — Confirm from county records
- **Building use classification** — Residential (single-family, multi-family), commercial, mixed-use
- **Prior permits or certificates** — Check if an EC exists; if so, note prior findings

### Building Diagram Determination

Using available information, identify the most likely building diagram before arrival:

- **Google Street View** — Assess foundation type and general shape
- **Building permits or plans** — If available from client or county records
- **Tax assessor photos** — Often available online
- **Site plans or deed** — May show crawlspace, pier, or slab details
- **Insurance adjuster notes** — If property has prior flood claims

This pre-identification helps you confirm the diagram in the field and ensures you gather the correct measurements.

### Elevation Datum Requirements

**Critical:** Determine the vertical datum requirements **before your visit**.

- **FIRM BFE datum** — Is it NGVD 29, NAVD 88, or another datum?
- **Local requirements** — Some communities specify a preferred datum
- **Building code datum** — May differ from FIRM
- **Conversion factor** — If you must convert between datums, document the source

Common conversion factors (consult your state DOT or USGS for local accuracy):

| Region | NGVD 29 to NAVD 88 Adjustment (feet) | Source |
|--------|---------------------------------------|--------|
| Eastern US | -0.5 to +0.3 | NOAA conversion |
| Central US | -0.3 to +0.1 | NOAA conversion |
| Western US | -0.1 to +0.3 | NOAA conversion |

*Note: Conversion factors vary by location. Always use NGS or NOAA published conversions for your specific area.*

### Benchmark Research

- **Identify published benchmarks** within 500–1000 feet of the property using NGS Datasheet tool
- **Note benchmark location** (bridge, utility pole, reference mark number)
- **Record benchmark elevation** and datum
- **Plan travel route** to benchmark and back (accessibility, public vs. private access)
- **If no published benchmark exists**, establish a temporary benchmark tied to a recognizable feature (top of mailbox post, edge of concrete pad, etc.) with clear photographs

## On-Site Procedures

### Step 1: Arrival and Property Verification

1. **Arrive on time and confirm access**
   - Meet property owner or representative
   - Confirm you have access to all areas (interior, crawlspace, garage, exterior)
   - Ask about hazards, gate codes, pets, or restricted areas
2. **Verify the property address** matches your EC form and FIRM data
3. **Take a wide-angle photograph** of the front facade and street address (for the EC)
4. **Review property sketch or aerial image** with owner if available

### Step 2: Building Type Confirmation and Diagram Selection

1. **Visually confirm the building diagram**
   - Foundation type (slab-on-grade, crawlspace, basement, elevated on piers/pilings)
   - Building shape (rectangular, L-shaped, complex)
   - Number of stories above ground
   - Presence of basement, crawlspace, garage, deck, or breakaway wall
2. **Match diagram number** from the reference guide
3. **Document any deviations** in field notes (e.g., "Diagram 7 — Standard Crawlspace, but house has split-level with sunken living room")
4. **Photograph the building** from multiple directions to confirm diagram

### Step 3: Benchmark Establishment and Vertical Control

1. **Locate your benchmark**
   - Set up GPS or total station to acquire reference coordinates
   - Tie to published benchmark if within working distance
   - If using temporary benchmark, measure and document with photographs
2. **Record benchmark elevation** (in the datum required for the EC)
3. **Establish vertical control** using auto level or total station
4. **Check your control** — Use two independent methods if possible (e.g., GPS and level run)
5. **Document all control data** in field notes with sketches and photographs

## Step 4: Measure All Required Elevations

The following elevations are required in Section C. **Measure each elevation from the established vertical control (benchmark).**

### C2.a — Top of Bottom Floor / Lowest Floor Slab

- **Slab-on-grade building**: Top surface of the concrete slab
- **Crawlspace building**: Top of rim band or top of foundation wall (if no rim band)
  - *Critical:* Do not measure top of joist or floor decking unless specified
  - Crawlspace buildings report the top of the bottom floor structure above grade
- **Basement building**: Top of basement floor slab or rim joist (depending on building code)
- **Elevated building (piers/pilings)**: Top of the bottom floor joists or platform
- **Measure in at least 3 locations** (corners and mid-point of long walls) and record the **lowest value**

### C2.b — Top of Next Higher Floor (if building has multiple stories)

- Only required for multi-story buildings
- Measure top of the second floor joists or rim band
- Measure in at least 2 locations; record the **lowest value**
- If building is single-story, leave blank or mark "N/A"

### C2.c — Bottom of Lowest Horizontal Structural Member

- **In Coastal High Hazard Zones (V zones and coastal A zones)**, this is often the critical measurement
- Identify the lowest horizontal structural member:
  - Rim band joist (if elevated)
  - Bottom chord of a truss (if exposed)
  - Bottom of roof sheathing (if rafters are exposed)
- Measure from the established benchmark to the lowest point
- **Record the lowest value** across the building footprint
- *Note: This measurement is especially critical for V zone properties*

### C2.d — Top of Garage Floor Slab (if attached garage)

- Measure top of the garage floor slab (or slab-equivalent)
- If garage is separate/detached, note "N/A" or leave blank
- If garage is integrated with main building slab, C2.d = C2.a
- **Measure in at least 2 locations** (corners); record the **lowest value**

### C2.e — Lowest Elevation of Mechanical and Electrical Systems

- **Inside building:**
  - Air conditioning compressor (indoor unit in attic, if applicable)
  - Furnace or heat pump bottom
  - Water heater
  - Electrical panel
  - Other equipment that could be damaged by flood
- **Outside building:**
  - Outdoor air conditioner compressor pad
  - Heat pump outdoor unit pad
  - Gas meter, propane tank bottom
  - HVAC unit pads
  - Any other utility/mechanical equipment
- **Measure all visible M&E** and record the **lowest elevation**
- **Document in Section D** which items were measured and their locations

### C2.f — Lowest Adjacent Grade (LAG)

- **Natural Grade**: Finished ground elevation before any improvements (typically not available; use pre-construction topo if available)
- **Finished Grade**: Current ground elevation after landscaping, grading, and improvements
- Measure grade at multiple points around the building perimeter (all four sides minimum)
- Record the **lowest value**
- **Measure grade within 1 foot of the foundation** — not 10 feet away
- **Note whether LAG is Natural or Finished** in the form
- *If crawlspace: LAG is ground surface; top of bottom floor is the rim band elevation*

### C2.g — Highest Adjacent Grade (HAG)

- Measure the **highest point of ground elevation** immediately adjacent to the building
- This may be a slope or high point on the property
- If property is relatively level, HAG ≈ LAG
- If property slopes upward on one side, HAG will be significantly higher
- **Document in Section D** where HAG was measured (e.g., "highest point on southeast corner")
- **Note whether HAG is Natural or Finished**

### C2.h — Lowest Elevation of Deck or Stairs Supported by Structure

- If building has an attached deck, porch, or stairs with structural support (ledger bolt attached to rim):
  - Measure top of lowest horizontal structural member of the deck (joist, beam, or slab)
- If stairs have independent footings (not attached to building), this may not apply — document in notes
- If no attached deck or structural stairs, leave blank or mark "N/A"
- **Measure at lowest point**; record lowest value

## Step 5: Crawlspace / Enclosure Details (if applicable)

If the building has a crawlspace or enclosed basement, complete the following:

### Crawlspace Square Footage (A8a)

- Measure length and width of crawlspace (or sketch and calculate)
- **Formula**: Length (ft) × Width (ft) = Crawlspace Area (sq ft)
- If crawlspace is irregular, divide into rectangles and sum
- Measure in at least one location to confirm depth is sufficient for measurement accuracy

### Flood Openings (A8b-f)

FEMA requires flood openings in crawlspace enclosures to equalize water pressure and reduce structural load during flooding. Non-engineered openings require a minimum of 2 openings per enclosure side.

#### A8b — Number of Openings on Lowest Side

- Count all openings (vents, grates, louvers) on the side closest to the flood source or lowest elevation
- Include openings with covers (grates, louvers, screens) if they provide net open area

#### A8c — Number of Openings on Side Perpendicular to Lowest Side

- Count openings on the side perpendicular to the lowest point
- Document both sides if property has multiple sides in floodplain

#### A8d — Bottom of Opening Height Above Adjacent Grade

- Measure from adjacent ground level to the **bottom of each opening** (not the top)
- If opening is a vent, measure to the bottom rim
- **Record the minimum height** if multiple openings at different elevations
- *This ensures water can enter during flooding without backing up against the building*

#### A8e — Net Open Area of All Openings (A8e1, A8e2, A8e3 - up to 3 openings)

- **Net open area** = Opening area minus any blockage (grates, louvers, screens)
- For a grate or louvre:
  - Measure the gross opening (W × H in inches)
  - Estimate net open area as % of gross (typical grate: 40–60% open)
  - **Formula**: Gross area × Net % = Net Open Area (sq in)
- FEMA requires minimum net open area per ASCE 24 or local floodplain administrator rules
  - Typical minimum: 1 sq in per 1 sq ft of crawlspace floor area
- List up to 3 largest openings; leave A8e3 blank if fewer than 3

#### A8f — Engineered or Non-Engineered

- **Engineered**: Flood openings designed by engineer, typically in plans
- **Non-Engineered**: Owner-installed or builder-standard vents
- Check if plans are available; if not, assume non-engineered
- Document in field notes if opening design is unclear

## Step 6: Attached Garage Details (if applicable)

Complete the following for any attached garage:

### A9a — Garage Square Footage

- Measure length and width of attached garage
- **Formula**: Length (ft) × Width (ft) = Garage Area (sq ft)
- If garage is detached, note "Detached" and leave A9 blank

### A9b-f — Flood Openings (same as crawlspace above)

- If garage has flood vents or door penetrations (typically a rollup door that does not seal):
  - Count openings
  - Measure height above grade
  - Calculate net open area
  - Indicate engineered vs. non-engineered
- If garage is above BFE or non-floodprone, openings may not be required — document assumption

## Step 7: Photography

Photographs are required on the EC form and must clearly show the building foundation and, if present, flood openings or vents.

### Minimum Photographic Coverage

- **Minimum 2 photographs** (ideally 4, one from each cardinal direction)
- **Required views**:
  - Front facade (facing street, showing foundation and entry)
  - Rear facade (showing back foundation and any vents)
  - Left side elevation
  - Right side elevation

### Photography Guidelines

- **Foundation must be clearly visible** — Show the connection between slab/stem wall and above-ground structure
- **Flood vents or openings** — Include close-up photo (within 10 feet) showing vent design and bottom height
- **Date and location** — Label each photo with date taken and view direction (Front, Left, Rear, Right)
- **Lighting** — Take photos in daylight; avoid backlit or shadowed foundation details
- **Wide angle** — Include enough context so reviewer can see the building type and surroundings

### Photo Organization for EC Submission

- Print or scan photos at 300 DPI for paper submission
- Label on the back: "Property Address — View Direction — Date"
- Attach to the back of Section C or as an appendix
- Digital submissions: Save as PDF or TIFF with clear file names

## Step 8: Special Conditions Documentation

Record any observations that affect the EC or that the reviewer should know about:

### Crawlspace / Enclosure Issues

- Crawlspace flooding history (staining, debris, moisture)
- Blocked or missing vents
- Improperly sealed or perforated openings
- Crawlspace condition (dry, wet, partially flooded)

### Split-Level or Sunken Rooms

- If building has rooms below the primary first-floor elevation:
  - Measure the floor elevation of the lowest room
  - Note if room is enclosed or open to main floor
  - Determine if BFE applies to this lower elevation
  - Document clearly in field notes and Section D comments

### Manufactured Home or Skirting

- If property is a manufactured home (mobile home):
  - Note if skirting is present (typically non-structural and removable)
  - Measure top of actual floor structure (above skirting if present)
  - Note if home is on a permanent foundation or block piers
- Skirting does NOT count as a permanent enclosure; document separately

### Breakaway Walls or Flexible Enclosures

- If building has a breakaway wall (designed to fail in flood):
  - Measure top of wall structure
  - Note if wall is engineered breakaway or flexible
  - Confirm engineered design documentation exists
  - Document in Section D

### Sloped Building or Irregular Footprint

- If building is on a sloped lot:
  - Measure C2.a (top of bottom floor) on uphill and downhill sides
  - Report the lowest value
  - Note in field notes which side is uphill/downhill
  - Document grade slope in sketches
- If building footprint is irregular:
  - Sketch the layout and note measurement points
  - Ensure lowest elevations are captured

### Access or Measurement Limitations

- If you cannot access crawlspace or garage:
  - Document why (locked, hazardous, inaccessible)
  - Note this limitation in Section D comments
  - If possible, request owner to open crawlspace or provide prior inspection reports
  - EC may need to be marked incomplete if critical elevations cannot be measured

### Utilities and Disconnects

- Note if natural gas, propane, electric, or water line enters the building below BFE
- Document in Section D as potential exposure
- This is not your responsibility to certify but is important context for homeowner

## Post-Field / Office Procedures

### Elevation Calculations and Datum Verification

1. **Transfer field data** into your EC spreadsheet or software
2. **Calculate all relative elevations** from your benchmark
3. **Verify datum consistency**:
   - If benchmark is NAVD 88 but BFE is NGVD 29, apply documented conversion factor
   - Cross-check at least two control points
   - Document conversion source in Section D
4. **Check for transposition errors** — Compare field notes to computed values
5. **Verify reasonableness**:
   - Are all elevations for a single building within expected range?
   - Are floor-to-floor heights reasonable (7–9 feet typical)?
   - Is M&E lower than top of floor (should be)?

### Building Diagram Selection Confirmation

- Review field photographs against possible diagrams
- Confirm:
  - Foundation type (slab, crawlspace, basement, elevated)
  - Number of stories
  - Presence of garage or enclosure
  - Any special features (split-level, breakaway wall)
- Select diagram that matches observations
- If no exact match, select closest and document deviation in Section D

### Flood Opening Validation

1. **Cross-check measurements**:
   - Is bottom of opening truly above adjacent grade?
   - Is net open area adequate (1 sq in per 1 sq ft of enclosure)?
2. **Review consistency**:
   - If non-engineered, are 2+ openings per side present?
   - If engineered, is design documentation available?
3. **Document findings** in field notes and Section D

### EC Form Completion (All Sections)

**Section A — Building Information**

- Owner name, address, tax parcel (pulled from research)
- Building address (verified on-site)
- Property coordinates (GPS)
- Building use (confirmed on-site)
- Building diagram number (from Step 2)
- Crawlspace sq ft, openings, net open area (from Step 5)
- Garage sq ft, openings, net open area (from Step 6)
- Photographs (see Step 7)

**Section B — FIRM and Flood Data**

- Community Name and ID (from FIRM research)
- County and State
- Map Panel and Suffix (current effective FIRM)
- FIRM Index Date and Panel Effective Date
- Flood Zone (from FIRM)
- BFE and datum (from FIRM or community)
- BFE source (FIS, FIRM, Community Determined, Other)
- LiMWA status
- CBRS/OPA status

**Section C — Elevation Data**

- Construction status (drawings, under construction, finished)
- Benchmark elevation and description (from Steps 3 & 4)
- Elevation datum (must match BFE datum; document conversion if used)
- All elevation measurements (C2.a through C2.h):
  - Fill in values in feet (or meters if specified)
  - Mark N/A if not applicable
  - Record measurement unit
- LAG and HAG with Natural/Finished designation
- Deck or stair elevation if applicable

**Section D — Certifier Information**

- Certifier name, professional license number, discipline
- Company name and contact info
- Signature, seal, and date
- Lat/long question answered (Did you obtain vertical control from surveyor? Yes/No)
- Comments:
  - Description of M&E systems measured
  - Explanation of any unusual findings
  - Verification that photos are attached
  - Datum conversion factor and source (if applicable)
  - Description of crawlspace condition, flooding history, or enclosure concerns
  - Any deviations from standard diagram or access limitations

### Quality Control Review (See QC Checklist)

Before releasing the EC:

- Use the QC Checklist (separate document) to verify all sections
- Confirm all required fields are complete
- Verify elevations are reasonable and consistent
- Check that photographs clearly show foundation
- Confirm certifier information and seal are present
- Review for legibility and professional appearance

### Prepare Distribution Copies

FEMA guidance recommends providing copies to:

1. **Community official** (floodplain administrator or building department)
2. **Insurance agent or insurance company** (if for rate reduction)
3. **Building owner** (retain original; provide copies to owner)

- Prepare three high-quality copies (laser printed or scanned)
- Include all pages and photographs
- Include any conversion factor documentation or engineering reports referenced in Section D

## Notes for Customization

Each firm should adapt this template to match:

- **Equipment used** (total station vs. GPS, auto level vs. digital level)
- **Preferred software** (CAD, surveying software, spreadsheet templates)
- **Regional conditions** (datum conversions, typical benchmark access, common building types)
- **Client preferences** (photo format, delivery method, report style)
- **Quality standards** (how many independent checks, who performs review)
- **Schedule and crew** (who collects data, who processes, who reviews and certifies)

Document your firm's specific procedures in an appendix or companion SOP document.
